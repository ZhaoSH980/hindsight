# Research memo

## Background
META has rallied approximately 18% in the two months leading into the as-of date of 2025-02-14, closing at $733.05 after reporting strong Q4 2024 results on January 29, including 21% year-over-year revenue growth and 50% EPS growth. The company guided Q1 2025 revenue to $39.5-41.8 billion (8-15% YoY growth) and announced a massive $60-65 billion 2025 capital expenditure plan driven by AI infrastructure investment. Reality Labs operating losses widened to $17.7 billion for full-year 2024, and management flagged ongoing regulatory headwinds in the EU and U.S.

## Bull case
META's Q4 2024 results demonstrated accelerating revenue growth (21% YoY) with expanding operating margins (48% in Q4 vs. 41% prior year), while the company's AI investments are already driving ad pricing higher (14% YoY increase in average price per ad). The server useful-life extension to 5.5 years will reduce 2025 depreciation by approximately $2.9 billion, providing a tailwind to operating income even as the company invests heavily in AI infrastructure. With DAP reaching 3.35 billion and strong ad impression growth, the core advertising business remains robust and supports continued multiple expansion.

## Bear case
META's 2025 capital expenditure guidance of $60-65 billion represents a dramatic increase from $39.2 billion in 2024, with infrastructure costs cited as the single largest driver of expense growth. Reality Labs losses continue to widen ($4.97 billion in Q4 alone), and the company explicitly warns of increasing legal and regulatory headwinds in the EU and U.S. that could significantly impact business and financial results. The stock has already rallied 18% in two months, pricing in much of the good news, while Q1 2025 guidance implies a meaningful deceleration to 8-15% revenue growth from the 21% posted in Q4 2024.

## Conclusion
META's fundamentals are strong with accelerating revenue and expanding margins, but the stock's rapid 18% two-month rally and the massive capex ramp create risk for a near-term pullback or consolidation. The Q1 2025 revenue guidance implies growth deceleration that could temper momentum if macro conditions shift. Over a 40-trading-day horizon, the risk-reward appears balanced with modest upside likely but elevated volatility from the extended run-up.
