# Research memo

## Background
Meta reported strong Q4 2025 results on January 28, 2026, with revenue of $59.89B (+24% YoY) and full-year revenue of $201B (+22% YoY), driven by 18% ad impression growth and 6% higher average price per ad. However, Q4 costs surged 40% YoY, operating margin compressed from 48% to 41%, and the company guided to a massive 2026 capex range of $115-135B for AI infrastructure. The stock rallied to $737 on January 29 post-earnings but has since declined sharply to $639 by February 13, a ~13% pullback.

## Bull case
Meta's core advertising business remains exceptionally strong, with Q4 revenue growth of 24% and DAP reaching 3.58 billion (+7% YoY), demonstrating continued user engagement and monetization power. The company generated $115.8B in operating cash flow in 2025 and holds $81.59B in cash and marketable securities, providing substantial firepower to fund its AI ambitions. Q1 2026 revenue guidance of $53.5-56.5B implies continued strong double-digit growth, and the stock's ~13% pullback from its post-earnings high may present an attractive entry point given these fundamentals.

## Bear case
The massive step-up in 2026 capex to $115-135B (up from $72B in 2025) combined with full-year expense guidance of $162-169B (up from $117.7B, a ~40% increase) signals significant margin pressure ahead. Q4 operating margin already compressed 7 percentage points YoY to 41%, and Reality Labs continues to generate losses ($6B in Q4, $19.2B full-year 2025) with no near-term profitability expected. The company itself flagged material litigation risk from youth-related trials scheduled for 2026 that 'may ultimately result in a material loss,' and EU regulatory headwinds persist.

## Conclusion
Meta's fundamental advertising business remains robust with strong revenue growth and user engagement, but the market is clearly weighing the massive 2026 capex escalation and margin compression against future AI monetization. The ~13% pullback from post-earnings highs suggests investors are skeptical of the near-term returns on Meta Superintelligence Labs spending. Over a 40-trading-day horizon, the stock's direction likely depends on whether the market re-rates the AI investment thesis positively or continues to penalize the spending trajectory.
