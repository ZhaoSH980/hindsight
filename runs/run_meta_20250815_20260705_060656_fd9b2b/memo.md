# Research memo

## Background
META closed at $782.66 on August 15, 2025, up 11.84% over the prior ~42 trading days, driven by a strong Q2 earnings beat on July 30 that showed 22% year-over-year revenue growth to $47.52 billion and 3.48 billion DAP. The company guided Q3 2025 revenue to $47.5-50.5 billion and raised full-year capex to $64-72 billion, reflecting aggressive AI infrastructure investment including a $13.79 billion Scale AI stake. Reality Labs losses continue at ~$4.5 billion per quarter, and management warned of accelerating 2026 expense growth driven by infrastructure depreciation.

## Bull case
META's Q2 results demonstrate accelerating core advertising momentum—ad impressions grew 11% YoY and average price per ad rose 9%, producing $47.52 billion in revenue at a 43% operating margin. With Q3 guidance of $47.5-50.5 billion and $47.07 billion in cash and marketable securities, the company has the financial firepower to sustain AI investment while returning capital ($9.76 billion in Q2 buybacks plus dividends). The Scale AI investment positions META to benefit from the AI infrastructure buildout.

## Bear case
Capex of $64-72 billion in 2025 (up from $60-65 billion prior outlook) plus management's explicit warning that 2026 expense growth will accelerate above 2025 levels—driven by 'a sharp acceleration in depreciation expense growth'—threatens future margin compression. Reality Labs has lost $8.7 billion year-to-date with no clear path to profitability, and the $13.79 billion Scale AI investment plus $3.0 billion in additional VIE tender offers creates meaningful balance-sheet risk. The stock's 11.84% recent rally may have already priced in the strong Q2 print.

## Conclusion
META's fundamentals are strong with 22% revenue growth and robust cash generation, but the elevated capex trajectory and 2026 expense-growth warning create uncertainty about forward margins. The post-earnings rally to $782.66 likely reflects much of the good news, suggesting limited upside over the next 40 trading days. We expect modest positive returns but with downside risk if macro conditions or AI monetization concerns weigh on sentiment.
