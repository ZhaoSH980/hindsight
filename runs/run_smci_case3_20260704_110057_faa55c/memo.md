# Research memo

## Background
SMCI filed its delayed FY2024 10-K on February 25, 2025, regaining Nasdaq compliance with no restatements and a clean BDO audit opinion, removing the binary delisting risk that had suppressed the stock for six months. The stock has rallied roughly 90% from its early-February low of $26.85 to $51.11 as of February 26, driven by relief on compliance plus CEO Charles Liang's $40 billion FY2026 revenue target and Blackwell rack-scale systems entering full production. However, BDO issued an adverse opinion on internal controls, DOJ and SEC subpoenas remain open, the CFO search is unresolved, gross margins have compressed to ~12%, and FY2025 guidance was cut by ~$4 billion at the midpoint.

## Bull case
The existential overhang is fully resolved — no delisting, no restatements, BDO signed an unqualified opinion on the financials — reopening the stock to institutional buyers who were sidelined for two quarters. The demand backdrop is the strongest in company history: hyperscalers committed ~$320 billion in 2025 capex, Blackwell rack-scale systems (HGX B200, GB200 NVL72) are in full production, and Supermicro claims first-mover volume leadership in direct liquid cooling. The $40 billion FY26 target implies ~65% growth and management calls it conservative, while even the reduced FY25 guidance of $23.5-25.0 billion still represents ~60% growth over FY24's audited $14.94 billion. Loop Capital raised its target to $70 on the compliance news.

## Bear case
The stock at $51.11 trades roughly 46% above JPMorgan's raised $35 target, with the firm maintaining Underweight citing limited visibility into GPU supply constraints and a tougher competitive backdrop. Gross margin of 11.8-11.9% is far below the mid-teens of a year ago — the AI-server business being won is structurally lower-margin and contested by Dell, HPE, and ODM-direct suppliers. The $40 billion FY26 figure is a target, not guidance, and asks investors to believe revenue accelerates to ~65% growth even as FY25 decelerates. BDO issued an adverse opinion on internal controls, DOJ and SEC probes remain open, the CFO remains un-replaced, and $700 million in new convertible notes plus existing $1.7 billion in converts create dilution risk near the $61.06 conversion price.

## Conclusion
The compliance resolution is a genuine catalyst that removes binary downside risk and justifies a meaningful re-rating from distressed levels. However, the stock has already rallied ~90% in three weeks and now prices in substantial optimism around the $40B FY26 target, which is a non-guidance aspiration against compressed margins and unresolved governance issues. Near-term upside likely depends on Blackwell ramp execution and whether institutional re-positioning provides sustained buying pressure, but the risk-reward at $51 is balanced-to-cautious given the speed of the move and the open DOJ/SEC overhang.
