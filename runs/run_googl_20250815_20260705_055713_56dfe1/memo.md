# Research memo

## Background
Alphabet reported strong Q2 2025 results on July 23, with revenue growing 14% YoY to $96.4B and EPS up 22% to $2.31, driven by 32% growth in Google Cloud and double-digit growth across Search and YouTube. The stock has rallied ~15% over the past two months to $203.32 as of August 15, 2025, buoyed by robust AI momentum and a $108B Cloud revenue backlog. Management raised 2025 capex guidance to ~$85B, signaling heavy investment to meet demand, while regulatory scrutiny and antitrust litigation remain ongoing overhangs.

## Bull case
Google Cloud's 32% revenue growth to $13.6B, combined with a $108.2B remaining performance obligation backlog primarily tied to Cloud, signals durable multi-year revenue visibility that should continue to drive multiple expansion. With Cloud operating income more than doubling YoY to $2.8B and management explicitly increasing 2025 capex to ~$85B to meet demand, Alphabet is demonstrating that its AI investments are translating into both top-line acceleration and profit growth, supporting continued upward price momentum.

## Bear case
The stock has already rallied 15% in two months to $203, pricing in much of the Q2 beat, while Alphabet faces escalating capex of ~$85B in 2025 that could compress free cash flow—which already dropped to $5.3B in Q2 from $18.9B in Q1. Ongoing antitrust litigation, heightened regulatory scrutiny, and the pending Wiz acquisition integration introduce execution and legal risks that could cap near-term upside after such a strong run.

## Conclusion
GOOGL's fundamentals are strong with Cloud acceleration and a massive backlog, but the stock's 15% two-month rally likely limits near-term upside to modest gains as the market digests the Q2 results and weighs rising capex against growth. Over a 40-day horizon, the risk-reward appears balanced with a slight bullish tilt, though volatility may remain elevated as investors assess whether AI monetization can sustain the momentum. I expect a modestly positive return but with lower confidence given the extended run-up.
