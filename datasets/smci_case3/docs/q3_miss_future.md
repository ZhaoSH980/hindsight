# SMCI: what happened next (published AFTER as_of)

The compliance-day close of $51.11 on February 26 turned out to be the top. Supermicro shares never closed above it again in the following ten weeks.

The unwind started immediately. On February 27, the stock fell 16% to $42.95 as the AI-hardware complex sold off around Nvidia's January-quarter report (Nvidia itself dropped 8.5% despite beating estimates) and profit-taking hit the compliance rally. By March 3 the stock was at $36.07. Through March the Nasdaq slid into correction on tariff and growth fears, and Supermicro ground down with it — no company-specific disaster, just a high-beta, low-margin AI-hardware name deflating as the narrative premium bled out. The early-April "Liberation Day" tariff announcements then hit the most China/Taiwan-supply-chain-exposed names hardest: the stock closed at $29.82 on April 4 and touched a closing low of $29.51 on April 21 — 42% below the February 26 close. On the 40th trading day after February 26 (April 24), the stock closed at $35.81, down 29.9%.

Then the fundamentals confirmed what the tape had been saying. On April 29, Supermicro pre-announced a major third-quarter miss: net sales of $4.5-$4.6 billion versus the $5.0-$6.0 billion guided on February 11, and non-GAAP EPS of $0.29-$0.31 versus $0.46-$0.62 guided, blaming "delayed customer platform decisions" that pushed sales into Q4 and gross margin roughly 220 basis points below Q2 on inventory reserves for older-generation products and expedite costs. Shares fell another 11.5% the next day to $31.86.

The final May 6 print made it official: Q3 net sales of $4.60 billion, gross margin of 9.6% (from 11.8% in Q2 and 15.5% a year earlier), non-GAAP EPS of $0.31, and fiscal-2025 revenue guidance cut again — to $21.8-$22.6 billion, from the $23.5-$25.0 billion range set in February, which itself had been cut from $26-$30 billion. The $40 billion fiscal-2026 target, the centerpiece of the February rally, was not reiterated on the call; analysts openly questioned its credibility. The stock closed May 6 at $32.94, down 36% from the as-of date.

The February thesis — overhang cleared, hypergrowth resumes — was falsified on both legs within the outcome window: the growth guide was cut rather than raised, and the margin floor gave way. Every bullish direction claim anchored at the February 26 close lost money at every horizon from 1 to 40 trading days.
