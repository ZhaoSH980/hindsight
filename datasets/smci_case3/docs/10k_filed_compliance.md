# Supermicro files the delayed 10-K, regains Nasdaq compliance

Supermicro beat its deadline. The company filed its fiscal 2024 annual report on Form 10-K and its overdue quarterly reports for the first and second quarters of fiscal 2025 with the SEC on Tuesday, February 25 — the last day of the exception window Nasdaq granted in December — and said it is now current with its Exchange Act reporting obligations and in compliance with Nasdaq's filing requirements. Shares, which closed Tuesday at $45.54, jumped about 12% once the filings landed.

The substance of the filings largely matched the company's reassurances. There were no restatements of previously issued financial statements. New auditor BDO USA signed an unqualified opinion that the consolidated financial statements "present fairly, in all material respects" the company's financial position — the affirmation Ernst & Young declined to give when it resigned in October.

The caveats are real, though. BDO simultaneously issued an adverse opinion on the company's internal control over financial reporting as of June 30, 2024 — meaning the control environment, as distinct from the reported numbers, had deficiencies that could allow material misstatements to go undetected. Management laid out a remediation program. The 10-K also disclosed that the company received subpoenas from the Department of Justice and the Securities and Exchange Commission late last year seeking documents following the short-seller report; both matters remain open.

The now-audited fiscal 2024: net sales of $14.94 billion, up 110% from fiscal 2023's $7.12 billion. The second-quarter fiscal 2025 10-Q put final Q2 net sales at $5.68 billion, consistent with the February 11 preliminary range.

For the stock, the binary risk is resolved: no delisting, no restatement. Attention now turns back to fundamentals — the Blackwell transition, the March-quarter guide of $5.0-$6.0 billion, the path to the $40 billion fiscal-2026 target, and whether gross margins near 12% mark a floor or a waypoint lower.
