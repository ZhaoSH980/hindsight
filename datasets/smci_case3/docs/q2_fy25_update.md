# Q2 FY25 business update: guidance cut for FY25, $40 billion target for FY26

Supermicro released preliminary (unaudited) results for its second fiscal quarter ended December 31, 2024, alongside a business update — the company still cannot file audited statements while BDO works through the delayed fiscal-2024 audit.

The preliminary numbers: net sales of $5.6 to $5.7 billion, up 54% year over year at the midpoint; GAAP and non-GAAP gross margin of 11.8% to 11.9%; and non-GAAP diluted EPS of $0.58 to $0.60, up about 5% year over year. The growth is still exceptional by server-industry standards, but the shape is notable — revenue up 54% while EPS grows only 5%, the arithmetic of a gross margin that has compressed from the mid-teens a year ago as hyperscale AI-server deals get priced aggressively.

The forward numbers carried the headlines, in both directions. For fiscal 2025 (ending June), management cut revenue guidance to $23.5-$25.0 billion from the prior $26-$30 billion, citing the technology transition to Blackwell-generation products. But for fiscal 2026, CEO Charles Liang put a $40 billion revenue target on the table — roughly 65% above the midpoint of the new FY25 range — calling it a conservative estimate driven by DLC adoption and Blackwell rack-scale demand. On the call, Liang reiterated his view that a large share of new data centers will adopt direct liquid cooling and that Supermicro's datacenter building-block strategy positions it to capture that shift.

On the compliance question, management said it remains on track to file the delayed 10-K and the two overdue 10-Qs by Nasdaq's February 25 deadline.

Alongside the update, the company announced a private placement of $700 million of new 2.25% convertible senior notes due 2028, together with amendments to its existing 0.00% convertible notes due 2029 — fresh growth capital, at the cost of potential future dilution. Pricing terms are to be set in the coming days.
