# The bull case: overhang cleared, $40 billion in sight

For six months the Supermicro story was a governance story. As of last night it is a growth story again, and the bulls' arithmetic is straightforward.

The overhang is gone. The 10-K and both overdue 10-Qs are filed, Nasdaq compliance is confirmed, BDO signed a clean opinion on the financial statements, and there were no restatements — the exact outcome Hindenburg's report and Ernst & Young's resignation letter implied was unlikely. The delisting scenario that forced index funds and risk-averse institutions to the sidelines is off the table, reopening the stock to a class of buyers who could not touch it for two quarters. Shares closed Tuesday at $45.54 and jumped again in Wednesday's early trading on the compliance confirmation.

The demand backdrop is the strongest in the company's history. Hyperscalers have guided to roughly $320 billion of combined 2025 capex; Supermicro has Blackwell rack-scale systems — HGX B200 and GB200 NVL72 — in full production as of early February; and management says direct liquid cooling, where it claims first-mover volume leadership, is becoming the default for new AI data-center builds. CEO Charles Liang's $40 billion fiscal-2026 revenue target implies roughly 65% growth over the FY25 guidance midpoint, and he calls it conservative. Even the fiscal-2025 cut to $23.5-$25.0 billion still represents ~60% growth over fiscal 2024's audited $14.94 billion.

The Street is starting to re-rate. Loop Capital's Ananda Baruah reiterated a Buy and raised his price target to $70 on the compliance news, arguing the company remains a premier AI-server share gainer with the filing cloud removed. Bulls note the setup echoes early 2024, when Supermicro's speed-to-market on Hopper-generation systems produced triple-digit revenue growth — only now with a bigger TAM and a cooling-technology moat.

Risks acknowledged: gross margin near 12% is thin, the DOJ and SEC inquiries disclosed in the 10-K remain open, and a new CFO still needs to be hired. But with the existential risk resolved and Nvidia's earnings tonight expected to reconfirm the AI-infrastructure cycle, the bulls argue the path of least resistance is up.
