# NVDA Q4 FY2025 results recap

NVIDIA reported fourth-quarter fiscal 2025 results (quarter ended January 26, 2025) after the close on February 26. Revenue was a record $39.3 billion, up 12% sequentially and 78% year over year, beating the company's $37.5 billion guidance. Data Center revenue was $35.6 billion, up 16% sequentially and 93% year over year. Full-year fiscal 2025 revenue was $130.5 billion, up 114%, with Data Center contributing $115.2 billion, up 142%. GAAP and non-GAAP earnings per diluted share were both $0.89.

The quarter's headline was Blackwell: CFO Colette Kress said NVIDIA delivered $11.0 billion of Blackwell architecture revenue in the quarter, calling it the fastest product ramp in the company's history. Large cloud service providers represented approximately 50% of Data Center revenue. Data Center compute revenue was $32.6 billion, up 116% year over year; networking revenue was $3.0 billion, down 9%, as the business transitions from NVLink 8 with InfiniBand toward NVLink 72 with Spectrum-X. Gaming revenue was $2.5 billion, down 22% sequentially and 11% year over year on limited supply of both Blackwell and Ada GPUs.

Guidance for the first quarter of fiscal 2026 calls for revenue of $43.0 billion, plus or minus 2%, implying roughly 65% year-over-year growth but a continued deceleration in percentage terms. GAAP and non-GAAP gross margins are guided to 70.6% and 71.0%, respectively — down from the mid-70s a year earlier as the costlier, more complex Blackwell systems ramp. Management said gross margins should return to the mid-70s later in the fiscal year once the ramp matures.

Despite the beat, shares fell 8.5% on February 27. Investor concerns centered on the gross-margin trajectory, slowing sequential growth, potential export-control tightening toward China, and lingering questions — post-DeepSeek — about long-run AI compute intensity. Jensen Huang argued the opposite: reasoning models, he said, add a new scaling law that increases compute demand.
