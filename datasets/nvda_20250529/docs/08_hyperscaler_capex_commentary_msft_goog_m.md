# Hyperscaler capex commentary (MSFT/GOOG/META Q1 calls)

The March-quarter earnings round from the big cloud buyers — Alphabet (April 24), Microsoft and Meta (April 30), and Amazon (May 1) — settled, at least for now, the question hanging over AI infrastructure names since the winter's data-center-lease scare: the 2025 budgets are intact, and one of them went up.

Meta raised its full-year 2025 capital-expenditure outlook to $64–72 billion, from $60–65 billion previously, citing additional data-center investments to support AI and higher expected costs of infrastructure hardware. Mark Zuckerberg framed the spend around AI now touching nearly every Meta product.

Alphabet reaffirmed its plan to invest approximately $75 billion in capex this year, weighted toward servers and data centers, even as management acknowledged tariff-related macro uncertainty in the ads business.

Microsoft reported $21.4 billion of quarterly capital expenditure including finance leases and remains on a roughly $80 billion pace for its fiscal year ending in June. Azure growth re-accelerated to 33% year over year (35% in constant currency), with AI services contributing 16 points — and management again said AI capacity remains supply-constrained, with demand ahead of what Microsoft can stand up.

Amazon's first-quarter capex ran near $25 billion, consistent with commentary earlier in the year that 2025 investment would reach roughly $100 billion, the substantial majority for AWS and AI infrastructure.

Taken together, the four largest buyers now imply well over $300 billion of combined 2025 capex. For NVIDIA — which reports May 28 — the read-through is straightforward: the demand side of the AI trade did not blink this quarter. The bear case has to rest elsewhere: China export restrictions, gross-margin pressure from the Blackwell transition, or a capex digestion phase arriving in 2026 rather than 2025.
