# Semis macro: tariffs and export controls backdrop

April has stacked three separate policy risks on top of the semiconductor trade, and none of them is resolved.

**Tariffs.** The April 2 "reciprocal tariff" executive order imposed a 10% baseline duty on nearly all imports effective April 5, with higher country-specific rates from April 9. Semiconductors were carved out of the reciprocal tariffs, but the exemption is narrower than it sounds — most chips enter the U.S. embedded in servers, phones, and other downstream goods. On April 9 the administration paused the higher country rates for 90 days for most partners while escalating against China, where cumulative rates on Chinese goods reached 145%; Beijing retaliated to 125% on U.S. goods. April 11 guidance then excluded smartphones, computers, and semiconductor-manufacturing equipment from the reciprocal duties — a reprieve the administration immediately described as temporary.

**Sectoral tariffs pending.** The Commerce Department opened a Section 232 national-security investigation into imports of semiconductors, semiconductor-manufacturing equipment, and derivative products (initiated April 1, published in the Federal Register April 16, public comments due May 7). Section 232 is the same authority used for steel and autos; it is widely read as the vehicle for chip-specific tariffs that would survive the reciprocal-tariff carve-out.

**Export controls.** Mid-month, license requirements landed on NVIDIA's H20 and AMD's MI308 — the China-market AI accelerators each company designed to comply with the October 2023 rules — triggering multi-billion-dollar writedowns. The Biden-era "AI Diffusion" rule, with its worldwide licensing tiers, has a May 15 compliance date and its fate under the new administration is unclear.

For the AI supply chain — wafers from Taiwan, HBM from Korea, packaging and test across Asia-Pacific — the common thread is that policy, not demand, is setting the tape. Equipment and analog names are guiding cautiously; AI accelerator demand remains the conspicuous exception.
