# NVDA FY26Q1 results (published AFTER as_of)

NVIDIA reported first-quarter fiscal 2026 results (quarter ended April 27, 2025) after the close on May 28. Revenue was $44.1 billion, up 12% sequentially and 69% year over year, beating consensus near $43.3 billion. Data Center revenue was $39.1 billion, up 10% sequentially and 73% year over year.

The China hit came in smaller than feared. NVIDIA took a $4.5 billion charge for H20 excess inventory and purchase obligations — below the up-to-$5.5 billion flagged in the April 8-K. The company sold $4.6 billion of H20 product in the quarter before the April 9 license requirement took effect, and was unable to ship an additional $2.5 billion. GAAP and non-GAAP gross margins were 60.5% and 61.0%; excluding the charge, non-GAAP gross margin would have been 71.3%, in line with the original guide. GAAP EPS was $0.76 and non-GAAP EPS $0.81; excluding the charge and related tax impact, non-GAAP EPS would have been $0.96. Gaming revenue hit a record $3.8 billion, up 48% sequentially.

Guidance for the second quarter is $45.0 billion, plus or minus 2% — a figure management said absorbs a roughly $8.0 billion loss of H20 revenue from the export limitations. GAAP and non-GAAP gross margins are guided to 71.8% and 72.0%, with the company still targeting mid-70s exiting the year.

Jensen Huang said the GB200 NVL72 "thinking machine" is in full production across system makers and cloud providers, and that AI inference token generation has grown tenfold in a year. On the call, management was blunt about China: the $50 billion market there is "effectively closed" to U.S. industry under the current rules, and Huang argued the restrictions will accelerate domestic Chinese alternatives.

Shares rose roughly 4% in extended trading as investors looked past the China writedown to the underlying beat-and-raise on Blackwell demand.
