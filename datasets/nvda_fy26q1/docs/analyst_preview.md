# Street preview of FY26Q1

NVIDIA reports first-quarter fiscal 2026 results after the close on Wednesday, May 28. Consensus sits near $43.3 billion of revenue — just above the $43.0 billion (±2%) guide issued in February — with non-GAAP EPS estimates clustered around $0.88 but showing unusually wide dispersion, because analysts differ on how much of the up-to-$5.5 billion H20 charge lands in the quarter and how they treat it in adjusted numbers.

The five questions that matter:

1. **H20 accounting and run-rate.** How much H20 shipped into China before the April 9 license requirement, how large is the actual charge versus the $5.5 billion flagged in the 8-K, and what does guidance assume about licenses (the Street assumes none are granted)?

2. **The July-quarter guide.** Q2 absorbs a full quarter of lost China business; sell-side estimates of foregone H20 revenue run to several billion dollars per quarter. A guide near or above ~$45 billion despite that hit would confirm Blackwell demand is covering the China hole.

3. **Gross margin trough and recovery.** Q1 was guided to 71.0% non-GAAP before the charge; management has promised a return to the mid-70s late this fiscal year as Blackwell yields and mix improve.

4. **Rack-scale execution.** GB200 NVL72 shipment cadence, and the timing of the GB300 (Blackwell Ultra) transition slated for the second half.

5. **Sovereign AI.** The May 13 HUMAIN announcement (an initial 18,000 GB300s for Saudi AI factories, with buildouts up to 500 MW) and the Stargate UAE project unveiled during the Gulf trip give management a fresh demand narrative outside the big four hyperscalers.

Positioning: shares have round-tripped violently — from a January peak near $149 to a closing low near $94 in early April, back to the mid-$130s over the past week — leaving the stock roughly flat for the year. Options imply a mid-single-digit percentage move on the print.
